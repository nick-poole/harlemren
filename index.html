Embracing Legacy

At the intersection of tradition and trend, The New Harlem Renaissance emerges as a beacon of grooming excellence, embodying the spirit of the historic Harlem Renaissance in every cut, shave, and trim.

Built on the foundational values of precision, craftsmanship, and client satisfaction, The New Harlem Renaissance is a celebration of style, culture, and individuality. Here, each service is a homage to the timeless sophistication of the Harlem Renaissance, providing clientele with not just superior grooming services, but an opportunity to partake in a rich cultural legacy.

The Revival of Style

In 1920s Harlem, a cultural revolution was taking place, showcasing the vibrancy of Black culture and influencing everything from music and literature to fashion and style. Within this vibrant period, barbershops emerged as communal hubs, where styles weren't merely followed, but passionately created, serving as powerful symbols of African American entrepreneurship and self-expression.

Drawing inspiration from this iconic era, this brand sets itself apart by fusing old-world artistry with contemporary barbering techniques, creating a unique experience that caters to the modern, style-conscious individual while honoring the past.

The New Harlem Renaissance invites you to step into a world where the past and present converge, where style meets heritage. Experience the meticulous attention to detail, personalized services, and unmatched expertise that defines the brand. The New Harlem Renaissance isn't just about looking good, it's about feeling connected to a vibrant history while standing out in the modern world.

Kennard Perry, a NYC native and graduate of the esteemed Morgan State University, is the vision and drive behind The New Harlem Renaissance. Perry earned his Bachelor of Arts in Political Science and Government in 1997, but it was his passion for barbering that led him to create something truly unique. He currently manages the Nile Style Barbershop in Northeast Baltimore, bringing his vision and expertise to clients daily.

Perry sees the New Harlem Renaissance as a revival of the industry's core values: education, style, and unrivaled customer service. It echoes the era when a well-groomed man signified not only sophistication but was also a reservoir of knowledge, wisdom personified in the barber's chair. This is a return to the roots, placing the client at the heart of the industry once again. It is a tribute to the time when barbershops were not just businesses, but cultural epicenters of the community. Here, style is not merely a display of skills but also a testament to the barber's personal flair. It's the renaissance of the gentleman barber, a profession grounded in artistry and finesse.

The New Harlem Renaissance is a movement - a mindset - marking the dawn of a new era in barbering while preserving the rich legacy of the past. Kennard Perry
