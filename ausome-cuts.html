AUSOME CUTS

The Thairapy Experience for Neurodivergent Children

Traditional barbershops can be overwhelming for neurodivergent children—especially those with autism. Loud noises, bright lights, and unpredictable environments often make haircuts a stressful ordeal. AUSOME CUTS changes that. Kennard Perry brings a mobile, sensory-friendly barbershop directly to local Baltimore City schools, creating a calm, familiar, and supportive setting where kids feel safe and in control.

Why It Matters

Neurodivergent kids often face unique challenges in everyday spaces. Barbershops, in particular, can trigger sensory overload—making haircuts a stressful experience for both children and caregivers. By meeting children where they are and adapting the environment to fit their needs, AUSOME CUTS helps transform haircut day into something empowering.

Founder Kennard Perry created AUSOME CUTS with a mission: to support neurodivergent children while uplifting the communities that raised him. Baltimore schools are more than institutions—they’re ecosystems of growth, care, and potential. This program is designed to nurture that ecosystem from the inside out. Why AUSOME CUTS?

  • Sensory‑Friendly Environment: Quiet clippers, soft‑close tool cases, and gentle lighting minimize sensory overload.
  • Specialized Comfort Tools: Weighted barber capes and adaptive seating provide security and stability during each session.
  • School‑Based Convenience: On‑site service eliminates stressful travel; flexible scheduling fits seamlessly into the school day.
  • Custom Haircuts: Styles tailored to each child’s comfort and preferences.
  • Sensory Adaptations: Volume, pace, and environment adjusted based on a pre‑visit consultation.
  • Therapeutic Touchpoints: Optional breathing breaks and social stories prepare and soothe before, during, and after each session.
